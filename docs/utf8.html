UTF8 and Bytestring in Haskell

Text (and String) represent a sequence of unicode codepoints. These are numbers in the range of 0 to 0x10FFFF which have been assigned certain properties (including a name) by the unicode standard, such as U+0065 LATIN SMALL LETTER E, which, besides the name I listed, is a Lowercase Letter. UTF-8 has the neat property that all the codepoints that correspond to the ASCII characters (U+0000 to U+00FF) are encoded as single bytes identical to their ASCII representation. But any other codepoint is encoded as multiple bytes.
